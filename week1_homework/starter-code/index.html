My Favorite Flowers

Hydrangea

Scientific Name: Hydrangea arborescens

Hydrangeas are a popular option for their rich blooms and leaves that offer color throughout the year. This variety of Hydrangea is native to the Eastern United States, and it blooms profusely from early summer to fall. The large flowered-domes holding hundreds of tiny flowers open lime-green in color, gradually changing to creamy-white. The deciduous shrub is wide, and the green leaves gradually change to a buttery yellow in the fall. Adding plenty of color to your garden throughout the year.

English Lavender

Scientific Name: Lavandula angustifolia

Lavender is one of the most popular flowers for its fragrance, so plant it where you can enjoy it.This is a bushy shrub that consists of aromatic, gray-green leaves. It produces long stems that support short, dense spikes of fragrant, pale to deep purple blooms in summer. It requires well-drained soil and looks spectacular in hedges or as an edging to pathways. Plant this somewhere that you can appreciate the summery perfume. French or Spanish Lavender is sometimes better suited to warmer regions.
